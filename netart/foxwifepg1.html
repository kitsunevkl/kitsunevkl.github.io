The Demon Lord and the Foxwife

Part One


Back to landing

There once was a Lord with a wandering eye. His Lady-wife wept regularly at the red bridge over the river, for her husband might not be cruel but he was certainly faithless. One day a passing Fox witnessed the lady’s lament.

“Dear lady, why do you weep?” Asked the Fox.

“I weep, for my husband does not love me, and spends his nights in other beds.” The Lady cried.

“Then you should not love your husband either.” Said the Fox, a mischievous smirk on his snout. Before the Lady’s amazed eyes, he became a beautiful man, the only tell of his origins a long glossy tail of russet fur. And for one night, the Lady’s bed was warm indeed, with the Fox for company. .



The Lady bore her Lord-husband a beautiful snow child, with white hair and eyes like storm clouds. They were happy for a time, though the snow child was full of perhaps a bit more mischief than her parents expected. Like the Lord and his family before him, the snow child grew up unaffected by the cold. Frost grew on her lashes, and ice spilled from her fingertips when she was happy. Until the time came when the snow child was a snow lady, and old enough to be courted..
